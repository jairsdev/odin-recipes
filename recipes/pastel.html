Pastel

Description

A pastel (plural: pastéis) is a typical Brazilian fast-food dish, consisting of half-circle or rectangle-shaped thin crust pies with assorted fillings, fried in vegetable oil. The result is a crispy, brownish fried pie. The most common fillings are ground meat, mozzarella, catupiry, heart of palm, codfish, cream cheese, chicken and small shrimp. Pastéis with sweet fillings such as guava paste with Minas cheese, banana and chocolate also exist. The pastel is classified in Brazilian cuisine as a salgado (savoury snack). It is traditionally sold on the streets, in open-air marketplaces, or in fast-food shops known as pastelarias. Wikipedia

Ingredients

  • 500g of wheat flour
  • 1 tablespoon of oil
  • water to stitch
  • 1 teaspoon of salt
  • 1 tablespoon of brandy
  • frying oil
  • Filling
  • 250 g of minced meat
  • 2 boiled eggs
  • 1 onion
  • 1 clove of garlic
  • 1 tablespoon of butter
  • 100 g pitted olives
  • parsley and chive
  • salt
  • 1 pinch of black pepper

Steps

  1. In a bowl, place the flour, oil, brandy and salt.
  2. Mix with warm water.
  3. Mix with your hands, then knead well on a smooth surface.
  4. After kneading well, open portions of dough with the help of the roller (leave the dough very thin) use the stuffing.
  5. Fold the edges together and press down with the tip of a fork.
  6. Filling
  7. Place the meat in a pan and bring to a boil.
  8. Stir until completely dry and loose.
  9. Once this is done, add the butter, the beaten onion and the crushed garlic.
  10. Saute well, season with salt and pepper.
  11. Add the chopped olives, the eggs in pieces and a good portion of parsley and green onions, cut very thin.
  12. Leave for another 2 to 3 minutes on the fire and put it to cool.
